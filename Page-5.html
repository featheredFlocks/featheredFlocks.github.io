ORANGE HEAD THRUSH

The Orange-headed Thrush, also known as the Indian Blackbird, is a bird species that is found in the Indian subcontinent, including the Western Ghats where Dandeli and Sinhagad are located. They are known for their striking orange head and breast, with a dark brown back and wings.

Orange-headed Thrushes are omnivorous and feed on a variety of insects, worms, fruits, and berries. They forage on the forest floor, often scratching the leaf litter to uncover prey.

These birds are solitary and are often heard singing their melodious and flute-like songs during the breeding season, which usually occurs from March to June. The female builds a cup-shaped nest out of twigs, grass, and leaves, which is usually placed in a low bush or a tree. Both the male and female incubate the eggs and take care of the chicks.

The Orange-headed Thrush is a common bird species in its range and is not considered threatened. They are popular among birdwatchers and can be observed in birdwatching tours in the Western Ghats region of India. In Dandeli and Sinhagad, they can often be seen foraging on the forest floor or perched on low bushes or trees.


GREY WAGTAIL

The Grey Wagtail is a slender bird with a long, slim tail and a yellow underside. The back and wings are grey, and the head and neck are black. The bird has a distinctive bobbing gait and a long, thin beak that it uses to catch insects, small fish, and crustaceans.

In Pune, Grey Wagtails are commonly found near streams, rivers, and other water bodies. They are known for their lively and active behavior and are often seen flitting about on rocks and boulders in the shallow water. They can also be found near man-made water bodies like ponds and canals.

The Grey Wagtail is a migratory bird, and its movements are dependent on the availability of water and food. They typically breed between April and August and build their nests near waterfalls or fast-moving streams. The female lays 3-5 eggs that hatch after about 12-14 days, and the young birds fledge in around 14-16 days.

Overall, the Grey Wagtail is a delightful and charming bird that adds to the biodiversity of Pune and other areas where it is found.

OSPREY

The Osprey, also known as the Fish Eagle or Sea Hawk, is a large bird of prey that is found near water bodies throughout much of the world, including India. They are known for their distinctive appearance, which includes a brown and white body, a hooked beak, and sharp talons.

Ospreys are typically found near lakes, rivers, and coasts, where they feed primarily on fish. They have several adaptations that make them excellent fish hunters, including sharp talons for gripping and powerful wings for diving into the water. They are known to hover over the water before diving down to catch their prey with their talons.

Ospreys are usually solitary birds, but may form pairs during breeding season, which occurs from December to April in India. They build large stick nests on trees or man-made structures near water bodies, and lay 2-4 eggs. The chicks hatch after around 5-6 weeks and are fed by both parents for several months.

The Osprey is a migratory bird in India, and is seen in the country during the winter months, from October to March. They are often observed near large water bodies, such as reservoirs, rivers, and estuaries. In India, the Osprey is a protected species under the Wildlife Protection Act of 1972.

The Malabar Trogon is a threatened bird species in India and is listed as Near Threatened on the IUCN Red List.
