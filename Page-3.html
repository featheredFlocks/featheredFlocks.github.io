WHITE BELLIED FLYCATCHER

The White-bellied Blue Flycatcher, also known as the White-bellied Blue Robin, is a small passerine bird found in the Indian subcontinent, including in the forests and woodlands around Pune. It is a resident bird, meaning it is present in the area year-round.

Male White-bellied Blue Flycatchers have a deep blue head, back, wings, and tail, with a white belly and throat, and a rusty-red breast. Females are more subdued in color, with a grayish-blue head and back and a pale belly and throat. Both sexes have a distinctive white eye-ring and a small, hooked bill.

White-bellied Blue Flycatchers feed on insects, catching them on the wing or from vegetation. They prefer forested areas with a dense understory, and can often be found near streams and other water sources. They are known for their melodious song, which can be heard throughout the breeding season.

These birds are not considered to be globally threatened, although habitat loss and fragmentation can affect local populations. If you are interested in birdwatching in the Pune region, keep an eye out for the beautiful White-bellied Blue Flycatcher in the forested areas around the city.

DARK FRONTED BABBLER

The Dark-fronted Babbler is a small bird species found in the Indian subcontinent. They are mainly found in the Western Ghats, a mountain range that runs along the western coast of India.

These birds have a distinctive dark forehead and crown, with a reddish-brown body and a greyish-white underpart. They have a strong, curved beak and a long tail. The male and female have a similar appearance.

Dark-fronted Babblers are social birds and live in small groups of up to 10 birds. They are primarily insectivorous and feed on insects, spiders, and other invertebrates. They forage on the ground and in the undergrowth, using their strong bills to probe and dig through leaf litter and soil.

During the breeding season, which usually occurs from February to April, the female builds a cup-shaped nest out of twigs, leaves, and other plant materials, which is placed in a low bush or on the ground. Both the male and female incubate the eggs and take care of the chicks.

Dark-fronted Babblers are common in their range and are not considered threatened. They are popular among birdwatchers and can be observed in birdwatching tours in the Western Ghats region of India.
