EMERALD DOVE

The Emerald Dove, also known as the Green Dove, is a bird species that is found throughout Southeast Asia, including India. They are also found in parts of Australia.

As their name suggests, Emerald Doves have a beautiful emerald-green plumage on their wings and back, with a distinctive pinkish-brown head and neck. They have a slender body and a long tail. The male and female have a similar appearance, with the male having slightly brighter colors.

These doves are primarily frugivorous and feed on a variety of fruits and seeds, including berries, figs, and grains. They are often found in forested areas, where they forage on the forest floor or in trees.

Emerald Doves are monogamous and form pairs during the breeding season, which occurs from December to May in India. The female builds a flimsy nest out of twigs and leaves, which is usually placed in a tree or shrub. Both the male and female incubate the eggs and take care of the chicks.

The Emerald Dove is not considered threatened and is a common sight in many parts of India. They are also popular among birdwatchers and are often observed during birdwatching tours in places such as the Western Ghats, the Northeastern states, and the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.
